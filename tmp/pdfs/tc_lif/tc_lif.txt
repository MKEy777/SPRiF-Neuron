     Gradient Exploding Problem Analysis
We analyze the severity of the gradient exploding problem
concerning the TC-LIF initialized in our predefined region.
Specifically, recurrent SNNs are trained on the S-MNIST
dataset with TC-LIF models initialized by different (β1 , β2 )
in the second quadrant. Our analysis involves recording the
values of β before and after training and calculating the in-
finite norms of the partial derivatives between adjacent time
steps in the last hidden layer.
   The result reveals that except for the model that is initial-
ized at (-0.2, 0.8) with a convergent infinite norm of 2.36, the
remaining models initialized within the second quadrant ex-
hibit commendable performance on the test set. While an in-
finite norm of the partial derivative between successive time
steps exceeding 1 suggests the potential for the exploding
gradient problem during long-term BPTT, our results sug-
gest that values slightly above 1 for the infinite norm do not
notably impede convergence. Encouragingly, for the major-
ity of β1 and β2 initialized in the second quadrant, the corre-
